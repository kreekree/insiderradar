Why This Data Actually Moves Markets

Executives know things. That's the whole point. When a CEO decides to spend half a million dollars of their own money buying their company's stock at the current market price — not through options, not through awards, but by writing an actual check — that's one of the clearest signals in public markets. They didn't have to do it. They chose to.

Research published in the Journal of Financial Economics has consistently shown that clusters of large insider open-market purchases tend to precede positive stock performance over 6–12 month horizons. InsiderRadar surfaces these trades in real time, flagged the moment they clear the $500K threshold — the same threshold institutional compliance desks use to escalate for analyst review.

Important: this page only flags transaction code P (open-market purchase) — cash spent at market price, voluntarily, by the insider. Stock awards, option exercises, and tax withholdings are excluded because they carry zero signal. All data is pulled live from SEC EDGAR and is public within 48 hours of each trade.
