The Quarterly Disclosure Wall Street Pays to Watch

Congress passed Section 13(f) of the Securities Exchange Act in 1975 because institutional money was getting too large to operate in the dark. Any manager controlling $100M+ in US equities must file Form 13F-HR every quarter — listing every stock position, share count, and dollar value. The SEC publishes it publicly within 45 days. Hedge funds pay analysts to track this. You can do it here for free.

The delay is real — up to 45 days after quarter end, which can mean 135 days between what you see and today. The data is a snapshot, not a live feed. Use it to understand conviction and thesis, not to mirror trades tick-for-tick. A fund holding the same position for eight consecutive quarters is telling you something. A new entry from a known deep-value manager is worth understanding.

Critical limitation: 13F shows long equity only. No shorts, no non-US, no bonds. A fund could be net short a stock and still show it as a long. Use the insider trading tracker alongside 13F data — when insiders are buying and institutions are building positions, that's the confluence worth watching. Full explainer: how to read 13F filings.
