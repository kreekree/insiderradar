The Information Wall Street Doesn't Want You to Find

Here's something most retail investors don't know: every time a CEO, CFO, or board member buys or sells their own company's stock, they are legally required to file a disclosure with the SEC within 48 hours. It's called a Form 4 — and it's been sitting on a free government database since 1996. Hedge fund compliance desks monitor it daily. You probably weren't told it existed.

When a CEO spends their own money buying stock at market price, that's not a guess. That's conviction. They know the pipeline, the deal flow, the numbers before they're public. When multiple executives buy within the same 30-day window? That's a pattern worth watching. InsiderRadar flags it automatically — no $200/month subscription required.

Every data point on this site comes directly from the SEC EDGAR database — the same government source that institutional desks and activist funds pay analysts six figures to monitor. We just made it searchable, sortable, and free. Enter any US ticker above and see what the insiders are doing right now.

Trades are displayed with full context: date, insider name, title, transaction type (buy vs. sell vs. administrative), share count, price paid, and total value. Transactions flagged ⚠ UNUSUAL represent open-market purchases exceeding $500,000 — the kind of move that makes institutional analysts pay attention.
