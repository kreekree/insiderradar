About InsiderRadar

The insiders always knew. The law just made them tell you. We made it easy to find.

Why We Built This

Since 1996, the SEC has required every corporate insider to publicly disclose their trades within 48 hours. CEO buys. Board sells. CFO option exercises. It's all there on SEC EDGAR — free, public, updated near-daily. Hedge fund compliance desks monitor it every morning. Most retail investors have never heard of it.

Meanwhile, the platforms that make this data usable — WhaleWisdom, Fintel, BamSEC — charge $50 to $200 per month for access to information that is legally public and costs them nothing to obtain. That felt wrong. The information asymmetry between institutional and retail investors is already severe enough without adding a $200/month paywall to public government data.

InsiderRadar proxies the SEC's own APIs, parses the raw Form 4 and 13F XML filings, and puts the result in a clean, searchable interface. Free. No account. No upsell. You're no longer on the outside.

Data Sources

  • ● SEC EDGAR Form 4 Filings — sec.gov — All insider trade disclosures published by the SEC within 2 business days of each transaction.
  • ● SEC EDGAR 13F-HR Filings — sec.gov — Quarterly institutional holdings disclosures from investment managers with $100M+ AUM.
  • ● SEC Company Tickers API — sec.gov/files/company_tickers.json — Official ticker-to-CIK mapping maintained by the SEC.
  • ● SEC EDGAR Submissions API — data.sec.gov/submissions/ — Company-level filing index used to locate the most recent Form 4 and 13F documents.

Last data source verification: April 2026. No third-party data vendors are used. All data is sourced directly from official US government endpoints.
